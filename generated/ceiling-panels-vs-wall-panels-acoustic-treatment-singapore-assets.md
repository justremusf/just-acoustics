## Instagram Caption

Wall panels and ceiling panels solve the same broad problem, but they do it in different ways.

Wall panels are usually the best starting point when you want visible design control, feature walls, and strong side-reflection treatment.

Ceiling panels make more sense when walls are glass, covered with shelves, or visually too important to interrupt.

For many Singapore offices, restaurants, churches and studios, the best answer is not either/or. It is the right mix.

Send us photos of the room and we can point out where treatment will matter most.

## Reel Script (60s)

Hook: Ceiling panels or wall panels? The wrong answer can make your room look worse without fixing the echo.

Wall panels are best when you have usable wall space and want the treatment to become part of the design.

They help with side reflections, flutter echo and speech clarity.

Ceiling panels are useful when the walls are glass, blocked, or too visually important to cover.

They help add absorption without changing the wall design too much.

The real answer for many rooms is both: wall panels where reflections are strongest, ceiling panels where you need extra coverage.
