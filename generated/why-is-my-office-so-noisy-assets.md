## Reel Script (60s)

HOOK (0–3s): Your office isn't noisy because it's busy. It's noisy because it's reflective.

(3–10s): Glass walls. Concrete ceilings. Vinyl floors. Every single one reflects sound instead of absorbing it.

(10–20s): So when someone takes a call, you don't hear it once. You hear it two or three times as it bounces around the room.

(20–35s): This isn't a soundproofing problem. Soundproofing stops sound from entering or leaving a space. You need acoustic treatment — panels that absorb sound inside the room.

(35–50s): Start with the ceiling. It's the biggest reflective surface. Then treat glass-walled meeting rooms. Then buffer your loud zones like the pantry.
