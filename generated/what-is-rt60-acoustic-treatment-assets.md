## Instagram Caption

RT60 is the single most important number in acoustics — and most Singapore offices fail it badly.

It measures how long sound takes to decay by 60 dB after the source stops. In plain English: how echoey the room is.

Targets we work to:
• Meeting rooms: 0.4–0.5s
• Classrooms: 0.4–0.6s
• Restaurants: 0.5–0.7s
• Churches: 0.8–1.2s

Most untreated glass-and-concrete offices here measure 1.2–1.8s. That's why your calls sound hollow and meetings feel tiring.

The fix isn't soundproofing. It's absorption — panels placed where reflections actually happen.

Send us photos of your space and we'll tell you the likely RT60 and what it needs.
