## Instagram Caption

Bad acoustics in your room? It's almost never a soundproofing issue — it's reverberation. Hard walls, glass partitions and tiled floors bounce sound until speech turns to mush.

The fix: absorb, don't block.

• Treat the ceiling first (biggest win)
• Cover the first reflection points on side walls
• Aim for 15–25% surface coverage
• Use proper PET or fabric panels — not foam tiles
• Add rugs and curtains as bonus layers

In most Singapore offices and HDB rooms, 4–8 well-placed panels change everything.

## Reel Script (60s)

HOOK (0–3s): Clap your hands in your room. If it rings… your acoustics are broken.

(3–10s) That ring is reverberation — sound bouncing between glass, concrete and tiles. It's why your calls sound muddy and meetings feel tiring.

(10–20s) And no — thicker walls won't fix it. That's soundproofing. You need absorption.

(20–35s) Here's the fix in 3 moves:
1. Treat the ceiling first — biggest hard surface.
2. Panels on the side walls at ear level where sound first hits.
3. Cover 15–25% of hard surface area with proper PET or fabric panels.

(35–50s) Skip the foam tiles from online stores — they barely absorb speech frequencies. And don't treat only the wall behind your screen. It's the least useful spot.
